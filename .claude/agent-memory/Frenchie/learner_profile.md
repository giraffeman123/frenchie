## Writing Strengths
- **Emotional authenticity:** Despite grammatical errors, the learner conveys genuine feeling. Example: "Mon esprit a une déchirure et ce me remue profondément" (la détresse du travail) — the image is vivid even if grammatically imperfect.
- **Vocabulary integration:** The learner incorporates most required vocabulary words in each exercise, showing commitment to the task and growing lexical range.
- **Narrative instinct:** Short scenes have a beginning, trigger, and emotional conclusion, even if the ending is sometimes abrupt. Example: "Là-dedans, Il trouva autre chose." (le portrait) — effective cliffhanger.
- **Idiomatic risk-taking:** The learner attempts idiomatic constructions ("bouche bée", "broyer du noir", "avoir hâte", "à court d'haleine") which is a strong B1+ signal.
- **Dialogue:** The learner regularly uses dialogue, which shows narrative ambition beyond simple description.

## Voice/Style Observations
- Prefers short, punchy sentences over complex constructions.
- Tends toward internal monologue and stream-of-consciousness passages.
- Uses real-world personal material (coffee addiction, work stress, anxiety) — writing is confessional and immediate.
- Occasionally mixes first-person (je) and third-person (il/elle) within the same exercise, which suggests difficulty maintaining narrative point of view consistently.
- Sentence rhythm is uneven: alternates between very short fragments and run-on clauses.
